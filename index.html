How they met

Raj and Simran met in a train... No No come back, Aditya and Mallika met in a train, on the way to Vaishno Devi. Aditya our handsome lad caught Mallika's eye and she was sure to whisper the same to her best friend's ear. Aditya ofcourse saw Mallika and was smitten by her and asked their mutual friend for an introduction. A tiring travel and whole lot of drama later while returning back home in the train again, these two said their first hellos and then there was no end to that conversation. Aditya our hero poured out every bit of his life secrets and memories to Mallika and their heart to heart came to a close as the clock strikes 4. The magic of the night befell and their hearts melted to each other.
Few months and friends meets later they said their I Love You's and the rest is history.

Events

3rd December

Engagement and Cocktail Night 6PM - 12AM

This event is no way close to ethnic. The bride & groom would like to have some "shots before the knot". With booze flowing down the floor and the western attire on, everyone would be busy showing off their new moves on the stage which they have finely honed.

4th December

Haldi and Mehndi 11AM onwards

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together and a professional mehndi artist applies mehndi on the bride's hands and feet. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more.

5th December

Wedding 8PM onwards

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. The groom comes with the barat and is greeted by the bride side with some dhol of course as maximum number of guests would be attending this event. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.

  Dress code

Ugh... dress codes?

Haldi and Mehndi11 AM onwards

Ethnic indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid colors of yellow, orange, pink & green and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas or dhoti & kurta.

Engagement And Cocktail Night6pm - 12am

Indo-western
A semi-formal look that is contemporary & fashionable which is not too prim & proper is the best to pull off this night. Ladies can wear something like gowns or saree with dark colours and gentlemen can wear suits. You can see some pins for ladies & gents.

Wedding 8pm onwards

Indo-western
Ladies can flaunt their unconventional sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a jodhpuri, high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.
